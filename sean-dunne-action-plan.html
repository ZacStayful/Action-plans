✅ Section 4 — Your plan & next steps
Your questions — answered honestly
How do you handle the wood-burning hot tub and the operational load it creates?▼
Changeovers with a hot tub are more involved than a standard property — we know that. For the wood-burning stove hot tub, we'll discuss with you whether to continue using it or swap to a heated electric option during our onboarding call. A standard electric hot tub is operationally cleaner and actually boosts year-round demand significantly (it removes the 'peak season only' limitation). On the cleaning side, our professional cleaning team handles full changeovers including hot tub checks, restocking, and preparation. You won't be involved in any of this.
We both work full time — how hands-off is this really?▼
Completely hands-off for day-to-day operations. Once the property is live, your involvement is: approving access for your personal use via our Slack channel (with 72 hours notice), and reviewing your monthly income statement. Stayful handles every guest interaction, cleaning, maintenance coordination, and everything else. The Slack channel exists so you can see what's happening if you want to — but you don't need to act on anything unless there's a structural issue requiring owner decision-making.
What's the situation with Welsh holiday let regulations?▼
You're right to raise it. Wales introduced a 182-day occupancy requirement (not 50% — it was updated). However, properties where the owner has their primary residence on the same address are typically treated differently from pure investment lets, and there are specific exemptions. The honest answer is this is a grey area for your specific setup, and you should take legal or planning advice before committing. Stayful can provide data on achieved occupancy so you can assess compliance — but the regulatory responsibility rests with you as the property owner.
Can Stayful work around our plans to develop the site into a wellness retreat?▼
Yes — this is exactly the kind of arrangement we're set up for. You can block any dates in advance for personal use or commercial events (yoga retreats, wellbeing weeks), and Stayful markets and fills all the other dates. Some owners run Airbnb Experiences alongside their Stayful-managed listing. We'll discuss the practicalities during onboarding and set up your availability calendar accordingly. The key is giving reasonable notice for blocks so we can manage forward bookings.
What's the honest worst case if projections aren't hit?▼
The scenario model shows a worst case of £1,086/month net in the lowest month (January). Annualised, a worst-case year where you hit below-average occupancy and nightly rates every month would deliver around £13,000–15,000 net. That's still significantly above the long-let benchmark of £5,940/year. The main risks are: slow initial ranking on Airbnb (which affects Year 1), a genuinely poor peak season, and the regulatory position in Wales. None of these are catastrophic given no mortgage — but they're real variables.
What does the cosmetic refurbishment mean for the timeline?▼
You mentioned the 3-bed is structurally sound and needs cosmetic work. Stayful's onboarding starts from signing the agreement — we'll do an initial inspection to confirm what needs doing and advise on priorities. Given you're aiming for 1st September and are nomadic through August, the realistic approach is: agree in principle now, sign before 7th August to lock in the special offer, and use our follow-up call on 10th August to confirm onboarding details and photography scheduling.
