Your Questions — Answered Honestly
Can I control which type of guests stay at my property? ▼
You can absolutely set preferences and we use these in our listing strategy. For a Chester city-centre property, healthcare professionals are a natural and very strong guest segment — the Countess of Chester Hospital is a key demand driver. We target NHS and medical guests through Booking.com corporate accounts and direct channels alongside Airbnb. That said, Stayful and Airbnb platforms are open to all verified guests, and we can't guarantee a specific guest type for every booking. What we can guarantee is that every guest is verified, has a track record, and abides by our house rules. Most guests at city-centre properties like yours are working professionals and short-break leisure travellers.
What happens with council tax during the transition? ▼
When you switch to short-let, the council tax situation changes in two stages. Initially, you will likely be charged the council tax premium rate (which many councils apply to properties not used as a primary residence). After approximately 12 months of operation as a short-let property, you can apply to have it reclassified as a furnished holiday let, at which point it moves from council tax to business rates — and at that level of lettings income, you would typically qualify for 100% small business rate relief, meaning zero ongoing rates. The transition takes some admin, and we'll guide you through when the time comes.
What should I do with my mortgage before we start? ▼
This is entirely your decision and I'd strongly recommend speaking to a specialist mortgage broker. There are three options you mentioned: (1) Holiday let mortgage — designed for STL, lenders include Paragon and The Mortgage Works, typically interest-only options available; (2) Buy-to-let with a company structure — useful if you plan to expand, but adds accountancy overhead; (3) Clearing the remaining balance with a personal loan — gives you full flexibility with no lender consent required. Given you said you prefer simplicity and are not looking to scale, option 3 is worth exploring seriously if the balance is manageable. The remortgage date of September 2026 gives you a natural decision point. Whatever you decide, we can work around any mortgage structure as long as your lender permits short-letting — most do with consent.
What is the honest worst case if projections are not hit? ▼
Honestly: the worst case is Year 1 performance comes in at the low end of the range — around £600–£700/month net in early months while the listing is building. That's still better than your current lodger arrangement in most scenarios, and above what a straightforward long-let would yield. The risk is not losing money — Stayful takes a percentage of what you earn, not a flat fee, so if income is low, our costs are low too. The bigger risk would be the property sitting empty if the Chester market underperforms significantly. Chester is one of the more consistent markets we analyse — strong year-round demand and multiple demand drivers — but I won't pretend projections are guaranteed. They aren't. They're based on comparable properties and Chester market data. If you're genuinely uncertain about the income, the best thing is to review the case studies and judge for yourself.
What are the contract terms — can I exit easily? ▼
There's an initial 6-month fixed term, during which early exit carries a £1,000 fee. After that initial term, it moves to a rolling 3-month notice period — so you can give notice at any time and be free 3 months later. You can also review the full agreement at the link above. We added the fixed term because onboarding, photography, and getting a new property listed takes real investment from our side — but we think 6 months is a fair and reasonable period to demonstrate what we can do.
I'm away mid-June to end of July — does that affect anything? ▼
Not at all — your property continues to be fully managed while you're away, which is exactly the point. All communications go through our Slack channel and you'll receive monthly statements regardless of where you are. If you want to block any personal use dates before you leave, we can set those up in the calendar in advance. The only thing we'd ask is that you aren't unreachable for genuine emergencies — but day-to-day, you don't need to be involved.
