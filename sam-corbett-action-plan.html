What Drives Bookings in Chester

🏛️
Roman City Walls & Heritage
Chester has Britain's most complete Roman city walls, driving massive short-break tourism year-round. Couples and families visit specifically for the heritage experience — generating consistent midweek demand even in shoulder months.
🏇
Chester Racecourse
The Roodee is England's oldest operational racecourse. Race days run May through September, including the prestigious Chester Cup weekend. Race meet bookings block-fill nearby properties weeks in advance at premium rates.
🎄
Chester Christmas Markets
Consistently rated one of the UK's top Christmas markets (Nov–Dec), Chester attracts huge visitor numbers. December is a genuine peak month — properties near the city centre are fully booked weeks ahead at premium nightly rates.
🏥
Countess of Chester Hospital
One of the North West's largest district general hospitals — visiting staff, locum doctors, patients' families and medical contractors create year-round demand. NHS and healthcare guests are reliable, respectful, and repeat-booking.
🎓
University of Chester
Around 10,000 students plus visiting academics, parents on open days, and postgrad candidates generate steady weekday and weekend demand. Graduation season in summer adds significant occupancy spikes.
🛍️
Shopping & Leisure Destination
Chester's The Rows — the unique two-tier medieval shopping galleries — plus independent restaurants and bars make it a popular staycation destination. Weekend leisure demand is strong and consistent throughout the year.

Your Questions — Answered Honestly

Will I actually get the right type of guests — particularly NHS workers? ▼
Yes — Chester is an NHS-heavy city. The Countess of Chester Hospital drives consistent demand from locum doctors, nurses, and visiting medical staff who prefer self-catering accommodation to hotels. That said, we can't filter by profession — Airbnb doesn't allow that. What we can control is the listing positioning (proximity to hospital, professional tone, workspace mention) and guest vetting (reading profiles, checking reviews, setting expectations in the house rules). The vast majority of guests across our portfolio are professionals, families, and couples. Problem guests are rare, and AirCover exists for when things do go wrong.
What happens if a guest damages something or steals? ▼
This is the right question to ask. Here's what actually happens: Stayful takes pre and post photographs of every stay. If damage is found, we raise a claim through Airbnb's AirCover programme (£2.5M protection) before the guest's next booking pays out. If AirCover doesn't cover it, we pursue the guest's deposit. We manage the entire claims process — you never have to deal with Airbnb directly. The honest truth: most stays involve no damage at all. When it does happen, it's usually minor. Serious incidents are rare, and we've found AirCover resolves the majority.
What about council tax — I heard short-let properties get charged a premium? ▼
Initially yes — there can be a short-let council tax premium. However, once your property is formally classified as a commercial holiday let (which typically happens once you're over 70 nights booked per year), it transitions from council tax to business rates. At that point, Small Business Rate Relief means most properties pay zero. The transition typically takes around 12 months. In year 1, this is something to budget for. We'll explain the exact process for Chester once you're ready to move forward.
What is the honest worst case if the income projections aren't hit? ▼
Honest answer: in a genuinely poor year — say 45% occupancy instead of 65% — you'd be looking at roughly £7,000–£8,000 gross and £3,500–£4,000 net. That's still meaningful passive income, but yes, it would be well below projection. Chester's city centre location and year-round demand profile makes this scenario unlikely — but it's not impossible. The main risk factors would be a very poor listing quality, no reviews in year 1, or a macro event affecting tourism. Year 1 projections (at 80% of full run rate) already account for the slow start. If you need a specific income floor to make the mortgage work, tell us that number — we can look at what occupancy rate gets you there.
What are the contract terms and how do I exit if I need to? ▼
Six-month initial term. Early exit in that period incurs a £1,000 fee (covers onboarding costs). After six months, it's a rolling three-month notice period — either party can give notice. There's no long tie-in. If you want the property back for personal use, you give three months' notice and that's that. We do also have the right to terminate immediately if the property becomes unsuitable (repeated complaints, safety issues etc.) — that clause protects guests and our reputation, and it almost never gets used.
My mortgage remortgage is due in September — should I wait until that's resolved before signing? ▼
September is actually a natural milestone. Your friend moves out around the same time, which means October/November would be the realistic start date anyway. Getting the agreement signed before you head away in July isn't necessary — but it would allow us to get everything scoped and ready so that when your friend leaves, we can move quickly into setup and aim for the Chester Christmas market season (November/December), which is one of the most lucrative times of year for city centre properties here. No pressure — but the timeline actually lines up well if you want to be live by Christmas.
