Your questions — answered honestly
What are all the costs I need to know about? ▾
Stayful charges 16% + VAT of the gross rental revenue (after Airbnb's 3% platform fee, before costs of sale). There's also a monthly software fee of £42. Cleaning is typically charged to guests and flows through to the gross — you don't pay it separately. Additionally you'll need a key safe (~£60 one-time) and potentially a new photography shoot (~£250 if needed). The income figures in this plan are already net of all platform and management costs — what you see is what lands in your account.
How will I be kept informed about what's happening? ▾
You get a private Slack channel for day-to-day communication, real-time access to your booking calendar, a detailed income statement by the 5th of each month, and a quarterly video review call with Zac. Anything that needs your attention — a maintenance issue, a guest request, a bad review — comes to you immediately via Slack. You're never left guessing.
How long does it take to build up reviews on a new listing? ▾
Roughly 1 in 4 bookings results in a review. On a new listing, the first meaningful review count (10–15 reviews) typically takes 2–3 months. After 6 months, most properties have enough reviews to achieve strong search ranking. The ramp-up in income reflects this — early bookings are slightly discounted to accelerate review accumulation. Zac's approach is to get the listing flying early and price up as the review count builds.
What happens if the existing R2R operator's photos aren't suitable? ▾
Zac will review the current Airbnb listing photos and reviews first. If the existing photography is strong and reviews don't flag a mismatch with the property, Stayful will attempt to transfer the listing (and its history) rather than starting from scratch. If the photos need a refresh or the listing is being started fresh, Stayful arranges professional photography — cost is ~£250. Zac will advise after you share the listing link.
What's the honest worst case if projections aren't hit? ▾
Projections are based on comparable property data and are not guaranteed. In a poor Year 1 — slow review build, lower-than-expected demand — you might net £5,000–£6,500. Your mortgage (£450/month = £5,400/year) would still be covered, but there'd be limited surplus. The good news: your setup cost is minimal (key safe only if photos transfer), so you're not risking significant capital. If performance is genuinely below expectations at 6 months, you have the option to exit the agreement with notice.
What's the negotiation with the R2R operator about furnishings? ▾
The R2R operator currently has their furnishings in the property. Zac's recommendation is to negotiate with them to leave these in place when they vacate — this avoids you having to source and install replacement kit, and speeds up go-live significantly. Most R2R operators are open to this as it saves them the cost of removal and storage. It's worth having that conversation early.
