Your Questions — Answered Honestly
Q: Will the income actually be consistent month to month? ▼
Honestly — no, not perfectly consistent. Short-let income has seasonal variation. July is roughly 45% higher than January for a MK property. What you do get is predictability: you'll know the rough ranges by month, and you'll have live booking visibility so you can see 4–8 weeks ahead. The monthly statement arrives on the same date every month. Over a full 12-month cycle, the income significantly outperforms long-let — but you need to accept that some months (particularly Jan/Feb) will be quieter than others.
Q: What kind of guests will stay in my property? ▼
In Milton Keynes you'll see a mix: business travellers (mid-week, usually Mon–Thu), NHS/hospital visitors, people visiting family in the area, and weekend leisure guests. The £200 security deposit, Airbnb's ID verification, and AirCover protection provide multiple layers of security. In practice, the vast majority of Airbnb guests are entirely unremarkable — professionals who want a clean, comfortable place to sleep. Party bookings and problem guests exist but are rare and largely screened out by Airbnb's platform rules and our guest requirements.
Q: What is the honest worst case if projections are not hit? ▼
Honestly, the worst realistic scenario for a well-presented, well-located 2-bed in MK is around £10,000–11,000 net in Year 1. Our most comparable portfolio property (803 Eastbank Tower, Manchester city centre 1-bed) netted £35,917 — but that's a larger and higher-rated market. For a 2-bed in MK, even if occupancy comes in at 50% rather than 65%, you'd be looking at £1,000/month net in Year 1. That's still comparable to a modest long-let. The upside case is materially better; the downside case is still workable. And crucially — you retain the flexibility to sell the property at any time with 3 months notice after the initial 6-month term.
Q: Can I still use the property occasionally? ▼
Yes — the property is yours. You can block dates in the calendar for personal use with 72 hours notice, outside of any confirmed guest bookings. The main consideration is that peak months (June–August) are your highest-earning periods — personal use in July/August costs more in foregone income than in January. Most owners use the property during off-peak months (Nov–Feb) which has minimal impact on annual earnings. We'll set up your centralised calendar so you have full visibility and control.
Q: What happens if I get a good offer on the property? ▼
This is exactly why the SELL profile works well with short-letting. After the initial 6-month fixed term, you can terminate the agreement with 3 months written notice. We don't lock you in long-term. The property stays bookable up until agreed dates and you earn income throughout. We're transparent about this upfront — the agreement is designed for flexibility. We'd coordinate the wind-down of bookings around any agreed sale completion timeline.
