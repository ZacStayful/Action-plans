❓ Your Questions Answered Honestly
What happens to the locked areas — my office and the conservatory? ▼
We lock and document restricted areas before the listing goes live. This is completely standard — many properties have storage rooms, home offices or areas the owner keeps private. We photograph the locked areas at onboarding and they are clearly described in the listing as restricted access. Guests book knowing about them. Importantly, if a guest tries to access a locked area and causes damage, we have full photographic evidence to support any AirCover claim.
My conservatory has a leak — does that need to be fixed before listing? ▼
Yes, any active leak should be addressed before going live — both for guest safety and to avoid ongoing damage to the property. We can advise on contractors during the onboarding process. If the conservatory is a restricted area that guests never access, we can proceed while the repair is in progress, but we'd want it resolved before the first booking where possible. This is something we'll assess properly at the initial property visit.
Can you do weekly inspections between guest stays? ▼
Standard inspections are done at cleaning turnarounds, not separately scheduled weekly visits. For a 6-bedroom property, you'll typically have 2–4 stays per week, so the property is inspected regularly as part of the cleaning process. If you want additional scheduled visits beyond turnaround cleaning, these can be arranged on a quoted basis. We discuss what's practical at the kickoff call once we understand the booking volume.
What if the figures aren't achieved in Year 1? ▼
Year 1 is genuinely more conservative — the 80% figure we use reflects real ramp-up patterns as the listing builds reviews and the algorithm learns. If for some reason performance is below the £39,248 Year 1 projection, you're still ahead of long-let net (£27,000). The management agreement has no performance guarantee — Stayful does not guarantee income levels. What we do guarantee is active management, pricing optimisation, and transparent monthly reporting so you can see exactly what's happening.
What if I want to access the property while it's being managed? ▼
You always have access to the live booking calendar and can block any dates for personal access. We need 72 hours' notice via the Slack channel, and confirmed guest bookings always take priority. Emergency access can be arranged with immediate notice. You're never locked out — it's still your property.
What's the commitment and how do I get out if it's not working? ▼
The initial term is 6 months. During that period, early termination carries a £1,000 exit fee — this covers our onboarding investment and any platform positioning work done. After 6 months, it's rolling 3-month notice from either party. If the market genuinely underperforms and the evidence is there, we have an honest conversation — we don't trap owners in poorly performing arrangements.
