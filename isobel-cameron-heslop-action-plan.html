❓ Your Questions — Answered Honestly
Why did my current manager let a toilet leak cause £1,600 damage without acting?▼
This is exactly the kind of failure Stayful is structured to prevent. We log every maintenance issue in a shared tracker, send monthly maintenance reports via Slack, and have standing authorisation to resolve issues up to £300 immediately. For anything larger, we contact you the same day. A known leak left unresolved until it causes structural damage would not happen under our management — because issues are actioned proactively, not parked.
How does Stayful fix the listing identity problem — being lumped in with the building?▼
Your property gets its own individual listing — your address, your photos (not shared with the building), your guest reviews. A hotel-style group listing fundamentally limits your income potential: reviews don't attach to your unit, guests can't choose you specifically, and your ground floor terrace and parking never get the prominence they deserve. We list you as a standalone property across Airbnb, Booking.com and direct channels from day one.
What about the Wi-Fi? I've already had negative reviews because of the 4G router.▼
Good Wi-Fi is a hard pre-condition for going live — not optional. The 4G router needs to be replaced before we publish the listing. LSH are managing the fibre installation, so the priority is confirming that completion date. Your new individual listing starts fresh — those negative reviews were on the old grouped listing, not yours. We can also address connectivity directly in the listing description to set clear expectations.
Will Stayful actually be responsive, or will I face the same delays on repairs?▼
We can't guarantee every contractor will be instant — that's honest. What we can promise is transparency: issues are logged, you get a monthly maintenance report, and we chase on your behalf. We don't mark up contractor costs. And critically, if something urgent happens during a booking (like your fridge situation), we deal with it same-day — sourcing a temporary solution, managing the guest, and keeping you informed. The fridge-not-replaced-for-days scenario is an ops failure, not bad luck.
What's the honest worst case if projections aren't hit?▼
At the market average of 61% occupancy (below Stayful's target of 71%), you'd earn approximately £27,987 gross and ~£14,554 net. That's still more than double the long-term let return of £6,880. Hull's low competition — just 12 listings within 0.38km, averaging 23 reviews — means a well-managed individual listing gains traction faster than in saturated markets. The real floor is constrained by how quickly reviews build, which is why we target a June or July live date to hit peak season early.
How does the transition actually work in practice?▼
You give Residential Estates/Guests their contractual notice (typically 1 month). Stayful builds your new listing in parallel during that period — no access needed. Once the notice period ends, we collect keys, confirm the Wi-Fi is live, fit the key safe, and go live. No setup fees — your property is already furnished and photographed. The whole transition takes 4–6 weeks from signing to listing live.
