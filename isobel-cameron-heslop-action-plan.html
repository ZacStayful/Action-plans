Your Questions Answered
My current manager left a toilet leak unaddressed and caused £1,600 damage — wiping out an entire month. How does Stayful handle this? ▼
Every maintenance issue is logged immediately — whether reported by a guest or identified during changeover. Urgent items are actioned same day. Repairs up to £300 are authorised without calling you. For anything larger, I get quotes and come back to you for sign-off. I have a network of trusted contractors — not random call-outs. The situation you described — a toilet leak left unresolved long enough to cause £1,600 of damage — should simply never happen. That's an operational failure, not an acceptable risk of short-letting.
My property is currently in a hotel-style group listing and my photos are being used for the whole building. How is Stayful different? ▼
Stayful creates and manages a unique, individual listing for your specific apartment. Your photos, your reviews, your pricing, and your listing identity are 100% yours. Your ground floor terrace, your parking, your specific outlook — all prominently featured. Reviews attach to your property. Guests specifically choose your flat, not "an apartment in Middleton Chambers." This matters enormously for long-term occupancy and pricing power. A listing with its own identity and review history commands better placement in Airbnb's algorithm.
Stayful is London-based — how can you manage a property in Hull properly? ▼
The vast majority of short-let management is remote: pricing, communications, booking management, guest vetting, reporting — none of this requires physical presence. For physical requirements (cleaning, linen changes, check-in coordination), we use local vetted contractors. Our Manchester and Lincoln properties — both in this portfolio — deliver 68.7% and 64.2% occupancy without a local office. I addressed your concern about this specifically in writing before today's call. If you have specific follow-up questions on how our Hull operations work practically, I'm happy to go through them.
What is the £42/month software charge and why is it passed to owners? ▼
The software charge is the cost of the channel management and dynamic pricing platform (Hospitable/Pricelabs). This keeps your listing synchronised across Airbnb, Booking.com and direct booking — preventing double bookings and updating pricing automatically every day. We pass it at cost with no markup rather than absorbing it into a higher management fee. On a £30,696 gross property, it's £504/year — about 1.6% of annual revenue in exchange for automated multi-platform management and daily price optimisation.
The Wi-Fi has already caused negative reviews and I lost a relocation client because of it. What can actually be done? ▼
The 4G router situation is a genuine risk that needs resolving before going live. You mentioned LSH are managing the fibre installation — that's the right fix. Before we list, I'd want confirmation that fibre is active and the speed is adequate (at least 50 Mbps for reliable guest use). If the fibre isn't ready in time, I can arrange a higher-spec 5G router as a temporary measure — it's substantially more reliable than a standard 4G device and largely eliminates the problem for guests. I would rather delay the listing going live by a week than risk a negative review in the first 14 days.
What is the honest worst case if projections are not hit? ▼
Honest answer: projections are estimates based on Hull market data, not guarantees. If occupancy tracked your current 57% rather than the 71% target, Year 2 net would be around £12,800 — roughly what Year 1 looks like. Still £6,200 per year more than long-let. The comparable in this portfolio most relevant to a worst-case scenario is 7 Beechwood Mount in Leeds — it achieved only 50.4% occupancy in 2025 and still generated £25,782 net for the owner. Hull's low competition market (average 4.7 stars, very few professionally managed units) means the floor is higher than most owners expect.
