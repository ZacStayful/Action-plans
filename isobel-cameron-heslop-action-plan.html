What Drives Demand in Hull
🏥
NHS & Healthcare
Hull Royal Infirmary and Castle Hill Hospital together employ over 9,000 staff. Healthcare professionals on rotation, visiting consultants, and patient family stays generate consistent year-round demand that fills low and shoulder months.
🎓
University of Hull
The University of Hull has around 14,000 students. Visiting academics, parents during freshers and graduation, and conference attendees create predictable demand particularly in September and June.
💼
Corporate & Relocation
Siemens Gamesa's offshore wind hub and major port operations bring corporate contractors who prefer fully serviced apartments over hotels. Amazon and other distribution centres drive relocation bookings for 1–3 month stays — your highest-value guests.
⛵
Tourism & Waterfront
The Deep aquarium, Hull Old Town, and the Maritime Museum draw weekend visitors year-round. Hull's 2017 UK City of Culture legacy continues to boost tourist footfall, with summer festivals and events supporting peak rates.
🚆
Transport Hub
Hull Paragon Interchange connects the city by rail and coach. Your city centre location means guests don't need a car — a key appeal for both corporate and leisure travellers who book on Airbnb.
🅿️
Parking Advantage
Your property includes parking — uncommon for Hull city centre apartments. This is a genuine listing differentiator that allows premium pricing vs competing units without parking, particularly for guests driving to the area.

Your Questions Answered
My current manager left a toilet leak unaddressed and caused £1,600 damage. How does Stayful handle maintenance? ▼
Every maintenance issue is logged immediately when reported — either by a guest or identified in a changeover. Issues are batched where possible for cost efficiency, but anything urgent is actioned same-day. You're notified via Slack. For repairs up to £300, I authorise and arrange without waiting for your approval (as agreed in the contract). For anything larger, I get quotes and come to you. I also have a small network of trusted contractors in the areas we operate — not random call-outs. The way your current manager handled that leak is exactly the kind of thing that should never happen.
My property is currently in a hotel-style listing shared with the whole building. How is Stayful different? ▼
Stayful creates and manages a unique, individual listing for your specific apartment — not a building-wide listing. That means your photos, your reviews, your pricing, and your listing identity are 100% yours. Reviews are attached to your property. Your terrace and parking are highlighted as differentiators. Guests book your flat specifically, not "an apartment in Middleton Chambers." This makes a meaningful difference to occupancy, review quality, and long-term listing performance.
Stayful is based in London — how do you manage a property in Hull? ▼
The vast majority of short-let management is remote — pricing, communications, booking management, guest vetting, and reporting don't require physical presence in Hull. For physical requirements (cleaning, linen changes, check-in coordination), we use local, vetted contractors. In fact, our Manchester and Lincoln properties — both in this portfolio — perform to 68.7% and 64.2% occupancy respectively without a local office. The critical thing is reliable operations, not postcode proximity. We addressed your concern about this in writing before today's call and I'm happy to discuss it further.
What is the £42/month software charge and why is it passed to owners? ▼
The software charge is the cost of the channel management and dynamic pricing platform (currently Hospitable/Pricelabs). This is the tool that keeps your listing synchronised across Airbnb, Booking.com and direct booking — preventing double bookings and updating pricing automatically. We pass this cost at cost with no markup rather than absorbing it into a higher management fee — it's fairer to owners who would otherwise be paying for it whether they need the full feature set or not. On a £31k gross property, it's roughly 1.6% of annual revenue in exchange for automated multi-platform management.
What is the honest worst case if projections are not hit? ▼
Honest answer: projections are estimates, not guarantees. Hull's STL market is genuinely competitive — with a low review average (4.7 stars across comparable listings) and limited inventory, there's real opportunity. But if occupancy tracked your current 57% rather than the 71% target, Year 2 gross would be around £24,800 — which is still roughly your Year 1 conservative figure, and still materially better than long-let. The 7 Beechwood Mount case study in Leeds delivered 50.4% occupancy and still generated £25,782 net for the owner. The floor is higher than most owners expect.
The Wi-Fi situation is a problem — I've already had negative reviews because of it. What can Stayful do? ▼
The 4G router situation is a genuine listing risk — poor connectivity is one of the top reasons for negative Airbnb reviews and it directly affects your search ranking. Before going live, getting fibre installed is strongly recommended (you mentioned LSH are managing this). Once you're with Stayful, I'd want to confirm internet speed before the listing goes live. If fibre isn't installed in time, we can discuss a higher-spec 5G router as a stopgap — we've done this before and it largely resolves the problem. I'd rather delay going live a week than take a negative review in the first month.
