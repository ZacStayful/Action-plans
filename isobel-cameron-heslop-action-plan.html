Your Questions Answered
My current manager left a toilet leak unaddressed and it caused £1,600 damage — wiping out a full month. How does Stayful handle maintenance differently?▼
Every maintenance issue is logged immediately when reported — by a guest, a cleaner, or during a periodic check. Urgent items are actioned same day. Repairs up to £300 are authorised and arranged without calling you. Above that, I get quotes and come to you for sign-off. I have a network of trusted contractors, not random call-outs. The situation you described — a leak left unresolved long enough to cause £1,600 of structural damage — is an operational failure that should never happen. It doesn't happen here because issues get logged and acted on the day they are raised, not left for the next visit.
My photos are being used for the whole building and my reviews aren't specific to my flat. How is Stayful different?▼
Stayful creates an individual listing for your specific apartment — not a shared building entry. Your photos, your review history, your pricing, and your listing identity are entirely yours. Your ground floor terrace and parking are featured as headline differentiators — not buried across forty identical apartments. Guests book your flat specifically. Reviews attach to your property. Over time this builds a listing with real history and credibility, which compounds into better algorithm placement and better rates.
Stayful is London-based — how do you actually manage a property in Hull?▼
The vast majority of what makes a short-let work — pricing, guest communications, booking management, vetting, reporting, listing optimisation — is entirely remote. Physical requirements (cleaning, linen, check-in) use local vetted contractors. Our Manchester property (68.7% occupancy) and Lincoln property (64.2%) both perform well without a local office. I addressed your concern about this specifically in writing before today's call. If you have specific questions about how the Hull operation works in practice — who cleans, how check-in works, what happens if something breaks — I am happy to go through it in detail.
What is the £42/month software fee and why is it charged to owners?▼
The software fee covers the channel management and dynamic pricing platform (Hospitable / Pricelabs). This is what keeps your listing synchronised across Airbnb, Booking.com, and direct booking at all times — preventing double-bookings and automatically adjusting pricing daily based on demand. It is passed at cost with no markup. On a £30,696 gross property, it is £504 per year — about 1.6% of annual revenue in exchange for automated multi-platform management and daily price optimisation. The alternative would be to absorb it into a higher base management fee and charge everyone the same regardless of whether they use multi-platform or not — we chose the more transparent route.
The Wi-Fi situation has already cost me a relocation client and negative reviews. What actually changes?▼
Before we go live, fibre connectivity needs to be confirmed. You mentioned LSH are managing the installation — that is the right long-term fix. I would want to see a speed test confirming at least 50 Mbps before we list. If the fibre is not ready in time, we can arrange a high-spec 5G router as a temporary measure — substantially more reliable than a standard 4G device and it largely resolves the guest experience problem. I would rather hold the listing for a week than take a negative review in the first fortnight. Getting a negative review early, especially one specifically about connectivity, is very hard to recover from algorithmically. We do not rush the go-live date.
What is the honest worst case if the income projections are not hit?▼
If occupancy tracks your current 57% rather than the 71% target, Year 2 annual net would be around £12,800. That is still £6,200 per year more than long-let. The comparable in this portfolio most relevant to a worst-case scenario is 7 Beechwood Mount in Leeds — it achieved only 50.4% occupancy in 2025 and still generated £25,782 net for the owner. Hull has lower competition than Leeds, with an average listing rating of 4.7 stars and fewer professionally managed units — so the floor tends to be higher than leads expect. There is no scenario where switching to a properly managed individual listing produces a worse outcome than staying with a manager who has already cost you £1,600 in unrecovered damage and at least one high-value relocation client.
