What Drives Demand in Hull
🏥
Hull Royal Infirmary & NHS
Hull Royal Infirmary is one of the largest NHS hospitals in the Humber region, generating consistent demand from locum doctors, nurses and contractors on placement. Lambert Smith Hampton (LSH) and other relocation agencies actively place NHS workers in serviced accommodation — exactly what your property competes for.
🎓
University of Hull
The University of Hull attracts visiting academics, postgraduate researchers, and conference attendees throughout the year. Academic visit patterns are less seasonal than leisure travel, helping stabilise bookings in shoulder and winter months.
🚢
Cross-Humber Ferry & Port
Hull's P&O ferry terminal connects to Rotterdam and Zeebrugge. Business travellers, freight logistics workers, and maritime professionals regularly require overnight stays near the port. This creates a mid-week commercial occupancy baseline that leisure-focused markets don't benefit from.
🏗️
Infrastructure & Construction
Hull's ongoing city centre regeneration — a legacy of UK City of Culture 2017 — continues to attract contractors and project professionals. Multi-week stays from contractors are particularly valuable: they book longer, accept slightly lower nightly rates, and generate minimal wear and tear.
⚽
Sport & Events
Hull FC and Hull City AFC both play at home regularly throughout the season. The KC Stadium draws away fans, families, and visiting teams. The Bonus Arena hosts concerts and events that drive peak weekend occupancy, particularly in summer months.
📉
Low Competition Market
The meeting confirmed Hull's STL market is notably thin in terms of competition — averaging around 4.7 stars and just 19 reviews per property. A well-managed, well-photographed listing with individual identity stands out sharply. Your ground floor terrace is genuinely uncommon in the city centre.

Your Questions Answered
What happens if Stayful fails to address a maintenance issue the way my current manager did? ⌄
All maintenance issues are logged in our system the moment they're reported — timestamped and tracked. You see them in the Slack channel immediately. For anything up to £300, we act without needing your approval. For anything above that, you get a message with a quote before we proceed. The toilet leak scenario that cost you £1,600 would have been flagged, actioned and (if guest-caused) escalated through AirCover within days. We don't let things sit.
My property is already listed with another company — how does the transition work? ⌄
You give 1 month's written notice to Residential Estates/Guests. While that month runs, we create your new listing from scratch — your existing photos can be used or we can arrange new photography. Listings go live on Airbnb, Booking.com and direct booking channels simultaneously. The kickoff call with you covers every detail. We handle the technical transition; you handle the notice.
Is Hull actually a strong enough market to justify switching? My current income is already "broadly in line" with your projections. ⌄
Yes, and the data backs it. Hull's STL market is genuinely underpenetrated — average review counts of just 19 per property signal low saturation. Your current income being "broadly in line" with our gross projections is encouraging — it confirms the revenue potential is real. The switch isn't primarily about earning more gross income, it's about: individual listing identity, proper maintenance management, multi-platform distribution, and a management team that actually picks up the phone. The £1,600 damage incident and the lost LSH relocation client are both revenue losses that wouldn't have happened with us.
What is the honest worst case if Stayful's projections are not hit? ⌄
Honestly: Year 1 will ramp. The first 3 months of any new listing earn less than run-rate while reviews build. That's true of every listing we've ever launched. The worst realistic case is Year 1 coming in at 65–70% of projection (~£20,000–22,000 gross) rather than the 80% estimate — that still represents £10,000+ more than a typical Hull long-let income. The case studies in this document show our Leeds property (50% occupancy) still delivered £40,665 gross. Hull at 57% — which is what your current manager achieves — would still project £24,000+ gross with our pricing approach.
The Wi-Fi issue is still unresolved — LSH controls the fibre installation. What happens if this delays going live? ⌄
You need active broadband before we list — guests will rate the Wi-Fi and a bad connection directly costs reviews and revenue (you've already experienced this). If the LSH fibre installation is delayed, there are three options: (1) 4G home broadband router as a stopgap (Three/EE business SIM with unlimited data, ~£35/month) — not ideal but functional for most stays; (2) delay listing until fibre is confirmed; (3) proceed with the agreement now, begin the transition, and set a live date conditional on connectivity. We'd recommend getting this sorted before accepting bookings. It's worth a direct conversation with LSH to get a firm install date.
What does the 13% special offer actually save me, and what happens if I don't sign by 31 May? ⌄
At £31,000 gross, 13%+VAT = £4,836/year in management fees. Standard rate (15%+VAT) = £5,580/year. That's a saving of ~£744 per year — compounding over 3+ years of management that's real money. After 31 May, the rate returns to 15%+VAT. The offer isn't a pressure tactic — it's a genuine incentive to start the process before we go into our peak summer season, which is when a well-prepared listing generates its best returns.
