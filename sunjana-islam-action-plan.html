What Drives Demand in Heaton & Newcastle

Why Newcastle short-lets work, and specifically why NE6 (Heaton) sits in a strong position.

⚽

St James' Park & matchday demand

Newcastle United home games drive consistent peaks. Around 35–40 home fixtures across Premier League and cups each season — many families and travelling supporters book 1–2 nights.

🏥

Royal Victoria Infirmary & NHS Trust

The RVI is a major NHS hospital handling specialist referrals from across the North East. Steady demand from visiting family members and outpatients needing accommodation near appointments.

🏫

Newcastle & Northumbria Universities

Two large universities with combined ~50,000 students — visiting parents at open days, graduation weeks (June/July), and conferences fill rooms for parents and academic visitors.

🍺

Newcastle weekend city break

One of the UK's strongest weekend break destinations — Quayside, nightlife, food scene, Tyneside culture. Friday–Sunday demand consistent across most of the year.

🚉

Heaton Metro & transport links

Heaton sits one Metro stop from city centre and a short ride to Newcastle Central Station, Newcastle Airport, and the Quayside. Guests rate easy transit highly — boosts review scores and ranking.

💼

Newcastle Helix & business travel

The Helix development plus established financial/professional services in central Newcastle drive midweek corporate bookings — usually higher nightly rates and 2–4 night stays.

Your Questions — Answered Honestly

The concerns you raised on the call, answered directly.

How does Stayful compare to Pass The Keys?
Pass The Keys is a much larger, more nationally-spread operator — they handle a high volume of properties across the UK. The trade-off is that you're one of many. Stayful is deliberately smaller and selective — we don't take on properties we don't believe will hit projection, and you have direct line to me (Zac) personally. Compare the proposed net figures, response times, and what's actually included in the management fee. The honest test: ask both who you'll be speaking to when something goes wrong at 11pm on a Saturday.
Income consistency was my main worry — how stable is it really?
Honest answer: short-let income is more variable month-to-month than long-let. October and January will always be your quietest. But over a full year, the variability flattens out, and the annual total is roughly double a long-let. Look at the monthly table on this page — the floor isn't zero, it's around £1,840 gross in your quietest month. Dynamic pricing and multi-platform listing reduce the chance of a totally empty stretch.
What's the honest worst case if projections aren't hit?
Realistic worst case in Year 1: ~60% of projected gross, so roughly £22,000 gross / £11,500 net. That's still above your previous long-let net of ~£9,000. The thing that would cause that: poor early reviews, slow ranking, or major maintenance issues that take the listing offline. We mitigate with professional photography, careful first guests, and rapid response. The case studies on this page are real properties — the lowest occupancy of those four (50.4%, Burley Leeds) still returned £25,781 net. That's a useful realistic anchor.
My partner needs to be on board — what do they need to see?
Share this page with them. The income comparison, the case studies, and the FAQ should answer most of their questions. The two things partners typically want to confirm: (1) the property still belongs to you and Stayful operates around you — yes, you can block dates anytime; (2) what happens to the property condition over time — pre/post photography on every stay and £2.5M AirCover protection, which is documented evidence the property is being looked after.
What if I want my property back for personal use, or to switch back?
Block dates anytime in the calendar — typically with 2 weeks' notice so we can stop accepting new bookings for that period. If you want to stop short-letting entirely, the management agreement is terminable on 30 days' notice. Your property, your decision.
When can we go live?
Once renovation completes mid-July to early August, setup is around 2 weeks — beds in, smart thermostat, key safe, photography, then listing live. Realistic live date is late July / early August. Every week of delay past peak season is roughly £700 in foregone gross.
