❓ Your Questions Answered
What if income projections aren't met?⌄

Year 1 is expected to come in at around 80% of the full run rate — that's built into the numbers we showed you. Projections are based on comparable properties in the Nottingham area and carry real uncertainty, particularly in the first 3–6 months while reviews are building. Worst case: around 15% below projection during ramp-up, exactly as discussed on the call. We don't guarantee income. What we do guarantee is that Stayful has a financial interest in maximising your returns — we earn more when you earn more.

How do I block dates when I'm home?⌄

The simplest way is a Slack message — drop Zac a note with the dates you're back and he'll block them on the calendar before any bookings can land. You also have access to a live booking calendar where you can see all upcoming reservations. Confirmed bookings always take priority — so the earlier you block personal dates the better, especially during peak summer months.

What happens with my personal items?⌄

As discussed, the recommendation is to lock personal items, documents, and valuables in a single room or cupboard before the property goes live. Guests should never need access to that space, and we've had great success with owners doing exactly this. Stayful is not liable for personal belongings, so the locked room approach is genuinely the best protection. It's a one-time setup task before 16 August.

What's the actual commitment and how do I exit?⌄

Six-month initial fixed term. If you want to exit within that window, there's an early exit fee of £1,000. After the initial term, it's three months' written notice — that covers winding down any booked stays cleanly. The contract is on Slack within 24 hours of signing. You can also read the full agreement above — no hidden clauses.

How are guests vetted and what if there's damage?⌄

Every booking requires a security deposit (held before check-in instructions are sent) and ID is checked on every guest. For damage beyond the deposit, Stayful manages all AirCover claims up to £100,000 — you never need to deal with Airbnb directly. Pre and post stay photos are taken every turnover. As discussed, if claims are under £75 we may choose not to pursue to protect our review standing — but anything material is actioned immediately.

What's the honest worst case if it doesn't work?⌄

If the listing underperforms, Stayful can terminate the agreement if the property is deemed unsuitable (as per the contract). More likely, you'd choose to exit after the initial six-month term. At that point, your property is unencumbered — you can return it to long-let or sell. The key safe is already in place so the onboarding cost is minimal. The actual downside risk is a below-expectation first 6 months — that's it. No long-term lock-in after the initial period.
