Your Questions — Answered Honestly

My target is £1,000+/month profit after mortgage — will that actually be achievable?+

Year 1 puts you at approximately £800/month after a typical mortgage, which is below your target. That's the honest ramp-up picture. By Year 2, at the full run rate of £1,805/month net, you'd be above £1,000/month profit depending on your exact mortgage. The gap closes as direct bookings build (typically 30–40% of bookings are direct by Year 2, which improves your net margin further). The 5-bed configuration — once renovation is complete — will also sit in a higher ADR bracket than the 4-bed comparison we used, so the full run rate is likely to be better than shown here.

How do you ensure I get the right type of guests?+

Every guest is screened: verified ID, £200 security deposit, and automated house rules acceptance before keys are released. We also use Airbnb's verified ID system and have minimum stay requirements that naturally filter out lower-quality bookings. Properties near Coventry's universities and hospital attract a high proportion of professional and academic guests — contractors, visiting lecturers, families of hospital patients — which is exactly the profile you want. We also have instant message screening as part of the booking process.

How does the 6-month initial contract work?+

The agreement starts with a 6-month fixed term. Early exit within that period carries a £1,000 fee — it exists because we invest real time and money in setup, photography, and onboarding. After the 6-month term, it moves to a rolling 3-month notice period — either party can leave with 3 months' written notice, no penalty. In practice, most owners never use the exit because performance meets expectations, but you're not locked in forever.

What if a guest causes damage?+

Airbnb's AirCover provides up to £2.5 million in damage protection per booking. We take pre and post-stay photography of every single booking and process all claims on your behalf — you never need to deal with Airbnb directly. For claims under £300, we authorise repairs immediately without needing your sign-off. The £200 deposit also acts as a first-line deterrent. Guest-caused damage is always an owner risk, but Stayful's claims management record means recovery rates are significantly higher than self-managing owners typically achieve.

How does the 15% + VAT management fee work?+

The 15% + VAT fee is calculated on gross rental revenue after Airbnb's own platform fee (~3%) is deducted. There's also a £42/month software fee. Cleaning costs are collected from guests as part of the booking price — they're not deducted from your net. The net figures in this action plan already account for all of these deductions, so what you see is genuinely what lands in your account.

What is the honest worst case if projections are not hit?+

Genuinely? The most likely downside scenario is that Year 1 occupancy comes in around 45–50% rather than 58%, with net income closer to £14,000–£15,000 rather than £17,324. This is still substantially above the long-let alternative (£12,600). A worst-case scenario where the property consistently underperforms would most likely be due to a combination of factors: photography quality, pricing errors in early months, or a sudden oversupply of comparable properties. Stayful's dynamic pricing software and active management are designed to minimise this risk, but no manager can guarantee specific outcomes.
