🗺️ Your Expansion Markets — What Zac Covered

You're looking to grow a portfolio. Here's the honest rundown on the markets we discussed.

🟢 ManchesterStrong

Excellent year-round demand — MediaCityUK, universities, NHS trusts, and a massive corporate traveller base. Properties within 30 mins of the centre consistently outperform. High competition but strong enough demand to absorb it. One of Stayful's top-performing markets.

🟡 LiverpoolSelective

High weekend rates but weekday occupancy is weak — the market skews heavily toward leisure. Not a problem if the numbers stack. But a buyer needs to underwrite the deal on weekend demand alone; don't count on even midweek fill. Location within the city matters a lot.

🟢 LeedsStrong

Strong medical and legal sector (Leeds Teaching Hospitals NHS, multiple law firms). Good weekday corporate demand. University catchment in LS2/LS6 is well-established. Similar demand profile to Manchester but with less competition in secondary postcodes.

⚫ ScotlandRuled out

Short-let licensing is mandatory across Scotland, with annual fees and a formal application process. In some councils (Edinburgh, Glasgow) there are active restrictions and quotas. The admin overhead makes it structurally unattractive unless you're already established there.

General rule of thumb:

Cities within 30 minutes of a major hospital, a university, and a rail station consistently produce reliable occupancy. Transport links, not just tourism, are the foundation of a strong STL portfolio. That's why Stoke works — and why cities like Coventry, Leicester, Derby, and Sheffield follow the same logic.

What we provide is active management, transparent reporting, and a genuine incentive to perform (our revenue depends on yours).
