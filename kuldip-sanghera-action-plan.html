📍 What drives demand in Stoke-on-Trent

🏥
Harplands Hospital & NHS
Healthcare contractors at Harplands Hospital (psychiatric services, Hanley) generate consistent mid-week demand throughout the year. These guests typically book for multi-week placements and prefer professional, clean accommodation near the hospital.
🏗️
Manufacturing & Project Workers
The West Midlands and North Staffordshire corridor is a major manufacturing zone — JCB's HQ is 10 miles away in Rocester, Michelin has a major site in Stoke, and Bentley in Crewe is 20 miles away. Contractors on multi-week placements need reliable accommodation.
🎡
Alton Towers Resort
Alton Towers (13 miles from Hanley) runs 265+ events per year including Halloween Scarefest, fireworks nights and Christmas events. Families staying 1–3 nights regularly seek accommodation in Stoke — close enough to the resort while keeping costs down.
🎓
Keele & Staffordshire University
Keele University (5 miles) and Staffordshire University (Stoke campus) generate visiting academics, parents, conference delegates and interview candidates year-round — especially during term changeover periods when hotel availability is tight.
🚂
Stoke-on-Trent Station & M6
Stoke station connects London (1h45), Birmingham (40min) and Manchester (50min). The M6 corridor (junctions 15–16) makes Stoke a natural overnight stop. Guests visiting the Potteries Museum, Gladstone Pottery, and the broader ceramics heritage trail also contribute.
🏨
Scarce Supply of Quality STLs
Stoke-on-Trent remains underserved by professionally managed short-lets compared to cities like Manchester or Birmingham. Well-presented properties with strong listings command premium rates and maintain high occupancy due to limited competition.

❓ Your questions answered honestly

There is natural seasonal variation — that's the honest picture. For your property, the range goes from around £1,084 in January to £2,129 in August. Winter months are quieter, and that's reflected in the projections. What Stayful's dynamic pricing does is minimise the quiet-month floor — pricing to attract the healthcare contractors and project workers who still book in January. You won't get a flat £1,645 every month, but you also won't see zero. The annual average is what matters over time.
There are two categories. Stayful costs (already reflected in the net figures shown): our 13% + VAT management fee, a £42/month software fee, and cleaning costs which are collected from guests — these are all deducted before we calculate your net. Owner costs (your responsibility): utilities and WiFi (~£400/month as you estimated), council tax, and one-time setup items (smart thermostat ~£200, key safe ~£30). The setup/furnishing cost is separate — Zac is sending you a breakdown. Every cost is visible in your monthly statement.
Airbnb's AirCover provides up to £2.5M in host damage protection. For any damage that occurs, Stayful manages the claim process entirely — you never have to deal with Airbnb directly. We have pre-stay and post-stay photography documentation to support every claim. Minor wear and tear is normal and accepted; significant damage is pursued through AirCover or directly from the guest's deposit. You're kept informed immediately if anything material happens.
Nothing happens with the property until your tenants leave mid-to-late July — Stayful handles only the paperwork stage until then. Once you confirm the handover date, we plan the furnishing and setup visit immediately. Onboarding typically takes 7–14 working days after the property is clear and available. That targets a September listing, which is a solid shoulder month (£1,587 net at full run rate). October is actually one of your strongest months (~£1,928 net) thanks to Alton Towers half term, so getting live in September puts you in place for that peak.

The agreement requires three months' written notice to terminate (or one month with the early exit fee during the first six months). Stayful can work around viewings if needed — we just need reasonable advance notice to manage the booking calendar around them.
The honest worst case for a 2-bed in Stoke: occupancy drops to around 45–50% instead of 65%, meaning net income around £13,000–£14,500 in Year 2. That's still roughly £1,100–£1,200/month and still substantially above the £950/month long-let alternative. Year 1 (ramp year) could be as low as £11,000–£12,000 if bookings are slower to build — though Stayful's platform positioning and pricing actively works to avoid this. The only scenario where this doesn't work financially is if you need guaranteed rent to cover mortgage payments, and your property is unfurnished with no leasable income in the interim — which is why timing the tenant exit and setup correctly matters.
