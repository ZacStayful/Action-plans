📍 What Drives Bookings in York
⛪

York Minster & Heritage Tourism

York receives over 8 million visitors annually. The Minster — Northern Europe's largest Gothic cathedral — anchors year-round leisure demand. As a 15-minute walk from your door, guests come for the full city experience, from the Shambles to city walls.

🎓

University of York & York St John

Combined student population exceeds 24,000. Graduation ceremonies, open days, and parental visits generate consistent midweek demand throughout the academic year. University of York's Heslington campus is a short cycle ride from Tang Hall.

🏥

York & Scarborough NHS Trust

York Hospital on Wigginton Road is minutes away. Visiting consultants, clinical rotations, medical training placements, and relatives of patients in extended care generate reliable midweek bookings across all seasons — including the traditional slow months.

🏢

Business & Corporate Travel

York is home to Nestlé UK's headquarters, LNER (rail operator HQ), and the National Railway Museum. It's also the headquarters of multiple national firms. Corporate midweek stays from these employers drive a significant portion of off-peak occupancy.

🎪

Events Calendar — Year-Round

York Races (6 major race meetings), York Food Festival (September), Viking Festival (February), York Christmas Market (one of UK's top three, November–December). Each generates a significant demand spike. December is your second peak season.

❄️

Air Conditioning — Summer Advantage

Your property has AC — rare in York's residential stock. During July and August, when temperatures regularly exceed 25°C, AC is actively searched for on Airbnb. This gives you a filtering advantage over the majority of competing listings.

❓ Your Questions — Answered Honestly
The York market averages 4.8★ and 100+ reviews — won't my new listing earn significantly less? +

Yes, in Year 1. This is real, and we've already built it into the projections. Year 1 figures use 80% of the full run-rate — reflecting that new listings typically earn 10–20% below established competitors until you've accumulated 20+ reviews. By month 4–6, you're usually hitting 70–75% of the top rate. By Year 2, you're competing on equal footing. The Stayful case study for York (17 Park Crescent) achieved £34,726 net in its first full year, slightly ahead of its own original projection, partly because of the algorithm momentum from strong early reviews.

I have a smart electric door lock — how does that work with Stayful's access management? +

Stayful works with smart locks regularly. We'll use your existing app-controlled lock for guest access and set up a backup key safe for emergencies. The main thing to flag: you mentioned the battery needs monthly replacement, and the lock makes noise before dying. We'll monitor this remotely where possible and set reminders. Guests are always fully briefed on how to access the property before arrival — this won't create problems.

My personal belongings will be in the attic — is that okay? And clothes in the lockable wardrobe? +

Completely fine. The attic will be out of bounds for guests — we'll make this clear in the house rules. The lockable wardrobe in the office room is standard practice. Guests are given access only to the spaces they're renting. Your belongings are your own and Stayful takes no responsibility for personal items — they should be stored securely or removed. A note in the listing about the storage-only attic and locked wardrobe actually reassures guests about what's included.

What's the honest worst case if projections aren't hit? +

The genuine worst case for Year 1 would be 60–65% occupancy rather than 74%, dropping net income to roughly £19,000–£22,000 annually. That's still £10,000–£13,000 more per year than a long-let. Your property has genuine advantages — the AC is a filter that fewer than 10% of York listings can match, and parking removes a common objection. The more realistic downside scenario is a slow first 2–3 months, followed by a gradual climb to the projections. We won't pretend projections are guaranteed — they're based on comparable properties and carry the standard caveats.

How do I block dates for personal use when I come back from abroad? +

Simple: mark the dates as blocked in the calendar (Stayful gives you full access) and drop a message on Slack. We need at least 72 hours' notice to avoid disrupting confirmed bookings. For longer blocks (a week+), give us as much notice as possible so we can plan around them. We manage multiple properties with owners who come and go — it's built into how we operate. When you're back and want to stay, you just let us know and we treat it like any owner visit.

What happens if a guest causes damage? +

First line of protection is the guest security deposit collected at booking. Second is AirCover — Airbnb's £2.5M damage protection which Stayful activates on your behalf. We document the property before and after every stay with photographs. If damage occurs: Stayful raises the claim, handles all communication with Airbnb, and works to recover costs from the guest. You never have to deal with Airbnb directly. In rare cases where recovery fails, you carry the financial risk — but AirCover covers the vast majority of scenarios.
