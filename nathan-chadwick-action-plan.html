Your Questions — Answered Honestly
What's the honest worst case if projections aren't hit? ▼
The honest worst case for Rossendale Road is that Year 1 low-season months (Jan–Mar) generate around £750–£780 net — below your £1,100 monthly costs. You'd need to cover a shortfall of roughly £300–£350 in those months. Year 2, the worst projected month is £939 — still a small shortfall. The long-let comparison (realistic £694/month net) also doesn't cover your costs. So neither route is cash-flow positive in the first year against your specific mortgage. The STL route with Stayful gets you closer to breakeven sooner, and builds an asset (reviews, platform ranking) that compound over time. Short version: go in knowing year 1 may need some cash top-up in the low months.
My estate agent quoted £1,300–1,400/month for long-let — why is your LTL comparable lower? ▼
The BB11 postcode in Burnley has average 3-bed long-let achieved rents of £650–£750/month based on current listings. £1,300–1,400 is closer to the asking rent in premium areas, or may have been quoted aspirationally. The £8,327 annual LTL figure in our analysis reflects a more conservative achievable rent. If you were to achieve £1,350/month LTL, that would be approximately £14,580/year net after a 10% agent fee — which would exceed our Year 2 STL projection. We're being transparent: if you can genuinely let at £1,300+ to a reliable tenant, that's worth considering. The STL advantage is flexibility, no void risk, and the scale argument.
Can I test long-let for a month first, then switch to Stayful? ▼
Technically yes, but there are practical considerations. If you take on a tenant (even one month), you'll need a proper tenancy agreement — and a tenant isn't obligated to leave at the end of month one. Assured shorthold tenancy gives them protection. A "licencee" arrangement can work for very short periods but needs careful drafting. Stayful can typically onboard and get a listing live within 10–14 working days of signing. The delay risk: peak summer season is approaching, and the highest-earning months (June–August) would be gone if you spend May–July trialling LTL. Practically, the opportunity cost of delaying peak season launch is significant.
What if a Booking.com-style guest damages the property via Stayful? ▼
Stayful primarily lists on Airbnb, not Booking.com — and the reason is exactly what you experienced. Airbnb's AirCover programme provides up to £2.5 million in damage protection per stay. We collect a £200 deposit, verify guest ID, and photograph the property before and after every stay. If damage occurs, Stayful manages the AirCover claim for you — you don't deal with Airbnb directly. The contrast with Booking.com is significant: Booking.com offers minimal protection and recovery is your problem. By using Stayful (primarily Airbnb-based), you get the platform that actually pays out.
How does this work when I'm building a portfolio of 3–4 properties? ▼
This is exactly the scenario Stayful is built for. Each property gets its own Slack channel, calendar, and monthly statement. You get a single point of contact (Zac) rather than managing separate cleaners, separate key safes, separate guest issues per property. Many of our owners have 2–5 properties with us. The economics also improve: at 3+ properties, you may qualify for a discussion about portfolio fee rates. The pipeline you described — Rossendale Road, then a third house, then two more from the land — is a realistic 5-property portfolio over 18–24 months. That's the conversation worth having.
What are the contract termination terms? ▼
Initial 6-month fixed term — early exit fee of £1,000 within this period. After the 6-month mark, either party can terminate on 3 months' written notice via the Slack channel. Stayful can also terminate immediately if the property is deemed unsuitable (repeated complaints, safety issues etc.). The 6-month initial term is there because onboarding, photography, and listing setup represent real cost and effort — the £1,000 early exit is a fair pre-estimate of those costs, not a penalty.
