❓ Your questions — honest answers
Yes. There is a 6-month initial fixed term — this gives us time to build reviews and achieve the projected figures, which genuinely takes 6 months. After that, it's a rolling 3-month notice period. If you need to exit during the fixed term, there's an early exit fee of £1,000. We think that's fair given the setup investment we make. Once you're past the initial term, 3 months' written notice and you're free.
The worst realistic case is around £1,368/month (February-style performance every month), which is still £549 above long-let. If every single month performed like February — which won't happen — you'd net approximately £16,400/year. That's still 67% more than the long-let alternative. A catastrophic outcome (below long-let) would require sustained poor occupancy and pricing, which we manage actively to prevent.
Airbnb's AirCover provides up to £2.5M in damage protection. We handle the entire claims process on your behalf — you never deal with Airbnb directly. We photograph the property before and after every stay specifically to support claims. For amounts under £300, we'll action repairs immediately without waiting for a claim to process.
You get direct access to the calendar software. You can block any dates yourself — select the date range, mark as unavailable, and it's instantly blocked across Airbnb, Booking.com, and our direct site. No need to contact us first. Just let us know via Slack so we can ensure no cleaning is scheduled and any access arrangements are in place.
You're in the best position to assess that. Many landlords operate short-lets on properties with mortgages — it's very common. The key thing is that your mortgage will be paid off in about 3 months, which largely resolves the question. We'd always suggest checking your mortgage terms if you're unsure.
You'll receive a PDF statement via Slack on the 1st of every month. It shows every booking that ended that month, the gross amount, all deductions (platform fee, management fee, software, any maintenance costs), and the net payout. Payment is transferred between the 1st and 5th. You'll also have direct calendar visibility so you can cross-reference any booking.
You'll need to update your property insurance to a policy that covers short-let/holiday let use. Standard landlord policies don't cover this. We can't recommend specific insurers, but specialist short-let insurance is widely available. AirCover provides the guest damage protection layer — your insurance covers the structure, contents, and your liability.
