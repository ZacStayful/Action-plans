What Drives Demand Around Streat
🌊
Brighton Proximity
One of the UK's most-visited coastal cities sits just 10 miles south. Visitors seeking a peaceful rural base while enjoying Brighton by day make Streat an attractive option, particularly for groups and families who want space.
🌿
South Downs National Park
The property sits within one of England's most-loved national parks. Walking, cycling, and horse riding routes across chalk downland are accessible minutes from the door, driving year-round bookings.
🚂
London Weekend Market
Hassocks station (1 mile away) offers direct trains to London Victoria in under 55 minutes. This makes Streat prime territory for London weekenders craving countryside escapes — a large and repeat booking market.
🎭
Glyndebourne Opera Festival
The world-famous Glyndebourne Festival (June–August) is 8 miles away at Glynde. High-spending opera-goers actively seek quality rural accommodation — Streat is among the closest viable options.

Your Questions — Answered Directly
The income is only slightly more than my long-let — is it worth the change?+
Honest answer: at Year 1 rates (£30119) versus a managed long-let (£18857), you're making £11262 more per year, growing to £18790 at full run rate. But for you, the main drivers were flexibility (no fixed tenancy), full control over access, and the option to sell with short notice. Those don't show up in income figures. If control matters as much as income, short-let is the better fit regardless of the delta.
The property is on oil heating — does EPCR renewal cause any issues?+
Not for onboarding. The EPC for rental compliance just needs to show a minimum E rating — it isn't specific to oil. We'll review your existing certificate during onboarding and flag if renewal is needed before listing goes live. The oil boiler needs an annual service and carbon monoxide detectors (which you likely already have). Stayful oversees this as part of the compliance checklist.
What if projections aren't hit in Year 1?+
The Year 1 figure of £30119 already assumes an 80% ramp — it's deliberately conservative. New listings take 3–6 months to build reviews and reach full visibility. A softer Year 1 might deliver 65–70% of projection in the first months, building to 80–100% by month 9–12. Even in a weaker scenario, Year 1 should comfortably beat the long-let floor of £18857.
I haven't used Slack before — is it complicated?+
Think of it as WhatsApp for business. You get a dedicated channel — just you and the Stayful team. We send booking notifications, monthly income summaries, and property updates. You reply when you want to. It runs as an app on your phone. Most owners are comfortable within the first week. If you prefer email for formal notices, that's fine too.
What does the linen service cover — and what do I need to provide?+
Stayful's linen hire covers fresh bed linen and towels for every changeover, plus consumables: tea, coffee, toiletries, toilet roll, and cleaning products. You need to provide duvets and pillows (4 sets minimum for 4 beds). Your existing sheets can stay for the photography shoot; the linen service takes over from the first guest. There are no hidden linen costs to you — it's priced into the gross revenue calculation.
If I decide to sell, what happens to the agreement?+
After the initial six-month term, three months' written notice ends the agreement. If you want to sell during the initial term, options include continuing management while marketing (STL income can actually support sale value), paying the £1,000 early exit fee, or speaking to Zac directly — these situations are usually handled sensibly. The agreement is designed to protect both parties' reasonable interests, not trap owners with genuine reasons to exit.
