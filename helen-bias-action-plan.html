Section 2 — The Opportunity in Staffordshire Moorlands
What Drives Bookings in Your Area
🏔️
Peak District National Park
Waterhouses sits at the gateway to the Peak District — England's most visited National Park. Millions of walkers, cyclists, and outdoor enthusiasts come to this area each year, creating consistent year-round demand for large group accommodation.
🌊
Dovedale & Thorpe Cloud
One of England's most iconic gorge walks — Dovedale — is a 5-minute drive from the property. It attracts over 500,000 visitors annually. Groups visiting Dovedale specifically seek large accommodation close by, and options at this capacity are extremely limited.
🚴
Manifold Track Cycling & Walking
The famous Manifold Track trail starts in Waterhouses itself. Cyclists, families, and walkers make this a destination in its own right. The property's large car park is a genuine asset for guests arriving with bikes, trailers, or multiple vehicles.
🎡
Alton Towers
One of the UK's biggest theme parks is just 10 miles away. Large family groups and friend reunions visiting Alton Towers need to sleep 10–12+ people — exactly the capacity The George offers. This drives significant summer and school holiday bookings.
🎉
Events & Celebrations
The property's unique features — massive beer garden, walled garden, and large car park — make it ideal for milestone celebrations, family reunions, and walking/cycling group trips. The George has genuine event-venue appeal that most STL properties can't match.
🏙️
City Escape Market
Waterhouses is within 1–1.5 hours of Birmingham, Manchester, and Sheffield — three major cities with combined populations of millions. The rural escape demand from these cities is enormous, and large group properties in the Staffordshire Moorlands are in short supply.

❓ Your Questions — Answered Honestly
How does remote management actually work when I'm in the US? +

Everything runs through your dedicated Slack channel. Your Stayful team handles all guest contact — you never deal with guests directly. If anything requires your input (above the £300 maintenance threshold, or a significant property decision), we send you a message. Most owners in the US check in once a week. You have full visibility via the booking calendar and monthly statements. The 6-year track record across 80 properties includes multiple overseas owners.

What if I want to use the property myself while I'm back in the UK? +

You can block personal use weeks directly in the booking calendar with 72 hours' notice, subject to no confirmed bookings in that window. Confirmed guest bookings always take priority — guests can't be displaced. The key is to block your preferred weeks as early as possible to prevent bookings from filling them. Peak summer weeks block quickly, so if you know you want August free, block it the moment the agreement is signed.

You mentioned VRBO — why don't you list there as well? +

VRBO has genuine strengths, particularly for US-origin bookings and some off-season markets. We made a deliberate decision 3 years ago to concentrate review volume on three platforms (Airbnb, Booking.com, direct) rather than diluting it across six. A property with 80 reviews on Airbnb converts at a significantly higher rate than one with 20 reviews spread across four platforms. If you have strong feelings about VRBO we can discuss it specifically for The George, but our default approach is to build depth on fewer channels.

What is the honest worst case if projections are not hit? +

Genuinely — Year 1 at 60% of projection would mean roughly £23,000–£28,000 net to you. That's a meaningful income from a property that might otherwise sit empty. The risk factors specific to The George: large group properties take longer to accumulate reviews (you need 70-80 for the algorithm to fully trust you, and large group bookings are less frequent than smaller apartments), and there's no LTL comparison here given the property type. We've never managed a property quite like The George in our portfolio, and I want to be upfront about that. The comparable data from similar-sized STL properties in the region and the existing demand drivers give us real confidence, but year 1 ramp is genuine.

How much control do I have over pricing? +

Stayful has full pricing authority as stated in the agreement — this allows us to optimise dynamically without needing approval for every rate change. However, you can set a minimum nightly rate floor below which we will never drop, and if you feel a specific date should be held at a premium (e.g. bank holidays you want to protect), we can discuss that. What you won't have is the ability to override rates day-to-day — that would undermine the dynamic pricing model and, based on experience, owners who intervene frequently tend to achieve lower revenues.

What happens at the end of the contract if I want to stop? +

The agreement has an initial 6-month fixed term. Early exit in that term incurs a £1,000 fee — this covers onboarding costs, photography, setup time, and any confirmed bookings that need to be managed or moved. After the initial term, either party can exit on 3 months' written notice. We'd never hold a property in a situation that wasn't working — if something is genuinely wrong we'll work with you to find a solution. But we do ask for the full notice period as there will typically be confirmed future bookings that need to be handled.
