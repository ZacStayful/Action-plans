Your Questions — Answered Honestly

What exactly does the damage policy cover — and what doesn't?▼

Airbnb's AirCover covers guest-caused damage up to £2.5 million per booking, including accidental damage, theft, and structural issues. Stayful manages the claims process entirely — photographing before and after each stay and filing any claims on your behalf. What it doesn't cover: general wear and tear (expected over time), your own belongings left at the property, and any damage you can't evidence with before/after photos. This is why we do a full photo inventory on every checkout. For anything under £300 where there's no coverage, Stayful will typically absorb the cost rather than invoice you — it's not worth the admin.

Who is responsible for the bins and waste collection?▼

Bin responsibilities stay with you as owner — you'll need to ensure the correct bins are at the kerb on collection day, or arrange a local keyholder who can do this. Your property manager (Stayful) handles everything inside the property, but bin collection logistics are a council matter and sit with the property owner. We'll confirm your bin collection days during onboarding so this is sorted before the first guest arrives.

What's the honest worst case if projections aren't hit?▼

Realistically, Year 1 underperformance would look like £8,000–£10,000 net rather than £11,948. This can happen if the listing takes longer than expected to rank, or if you go live outside peak season. At £8,000, you're still well above your current LTL net of £7,620 — so even in a poor first year, you're not worse off. We've never had a 2-bed property in the Stoke area deliver less than £7,500 net in Year 1 when it's been set up properly and launched at a sensible price point.

I already have building and contents insurance — do I need a new policy?▼

Yes, you'll need to update your policy or take out a new one that specifically covers short-term lets. Standard residential landlord or home insurance policies typically exclude holiday/STL use and won't pay out if a guest causes damage and you need to claim. The good news is STL-specific policies are widely available — companies like Pikl, Superhog, and Guardhog offer them. We'll give you a list of recommended providers. This is a one-time setup task that takes about 30 minutes.

What happens during the void between tenants leaving and the property going live?▼

Between your tenants moving out in July and the listing going live, there will be a setup period of 2–4 weeks. This is normal — photography needs to happen, the listing needs to be built and submitted for review, and the property needs to be in guest-ready condition. We'll agree the target live date during onboarding. For a July tenant departure, a late-July or early-August live date is very achievable — which means you catch peak season. That's why signing the agreement promptly matters.

The management fee is 13% — how does that compare to other STL companies?▼

Most full-service STL management companies in the UK charge between 15% and 25%. Stayful's standard rate is 15% + VAT. The 13% offer on the table is specifically locked to this conversation — if you come back in 3 months, the standard rate applies. At 13%+VAT, your management cost at full run rate is approximately £3,090/year, which is substantially less than even a basic long-let agent at 10-12% of £9,000 gross.
