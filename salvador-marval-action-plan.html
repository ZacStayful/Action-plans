🗺 What Drives Demand in Silverstone
🏎
Silverstone Circuit
The world-famous home of the British F1 Grand Prix and MotoGP. Race weekends bring tens of thousands of visitors who need nearby accommodation — typically at 3–5× normal rates. Your property is within minutes of the circuit, making it a premium option for travelling fans and team guests.
🔧
Motorsport Engineering Cluster
Brackley (Mercedes F1 HQ), Silverstone itself (Aston Martin F1 staff), Cosworth in Northampton — this is the global centre of motorsport. Engineers, contractors and corporate visitors need short-stay accommodation year-round, not just during race weekends.
🏗
HS2 Construction Workforce
The HS2 corridor runs close to Northamptonshire, creating sustained monthly demand from contractors on long, rolling bookings. These are ideal guests — organised, professional, not party-goers.
🌿
South Northamptonshire Tourism
Rolling countryside, stone villages, Silverstone's own museum and driving experiences, and easy access to the Cotswolds. Rural escape bookings from Birmingham, London and the Midlands are a growing category for Airbnb.
🏆
Year-Round Track Events
Beyond F1 and MotoGP, Silverstone hosts GT races, historics, British Touring Cars, formula academy rounds, and corporate track days across 9+ months of the year. Demand is not limited to two weekends.
📍
Central England Location
Silverstone sits near the geographic centre of England — 70 miles from London, 40 miles from Birmingham, 25 miles from Oxford. Travellers transiting between cities increasingly choose rural countryside stays over city hotels.

❓ Your Questions — Answered Honestly
I've had bad experiences with letting agents and R2R companies in the past — how is Stayful different?▼

That's one of the most common things we hear. Traditional letting agents have opaque fees and misaligned incentives — they get paid whether the property performs well or not. R2R operators often have no skin in the game with your property. Stayful's model is different: we only earn when you do. Our management fee is taken from rent collected, not invoiced separately. You have full visibility of every booking and every deduction in your monthly statement. No hidden charges. No subcontracted cleaners you never meet. We're a small, owner-managed business — Zac's name is on everything we send you.

What if the income projections aren't achieved?▼

Honest answer: Year 1 will be lower than Year 2. We've built that in — the figures above at 80% of full rate are deliberately conservative. Silverstone is a strong market but not every property hits projections immediately. If bookings are slow in the first 90 days, Zac will review pricing, photography and listing copy with you directly. We don't disappear after onboarding. The projections are also annual averages — some months (like January) will be low; others (March, May) will be strong.

Can I still sell the property if I decide to later?▼

Yes — and this was discussed on our call. After the 6-month fixed term, you're on a 3-month rolling notice period. You can serve notice to sell at any time. During that 3 months, viewings can be blocked or accommodated on the calendar without affecting the contract. The notice period exists to protect the guest pipeline — guests have already paid and booked; we need to honour those commitments. Three months is standard and is actually a selling point: it shows continuity of income right up to exchange.

What happens with my running costs — utilities, council tax, gardener?▼

Under STL management, you remain responsible for council tax, utilities and the gardener. What changes: you no longer need a cleaner (guests pay a cleaning fee that covers our professional changeover). Internet must stay active (guests need WiFi). Smart meters and a key safe are a one-time setup cost. Your running costs don't increase significantly — the cleaner saving roughly offsets the small increase in electricity from guest turnover.

What is the honest worst case if projections are not hit?▼

If the property performed at its absolute minimum — January-level income (£402/month Y2, £322/month Y1) every single month — annual net would be around £3,864/year. That is the floor, and it requires everything to go wrong simultaneously. More realistically, if the property underperforms by 30%, you'd see ~£11,000/year — still ahead of the long-let benchmark. The majority of Stayful properties hit or exceed Year 2 projections within 12 months. The Silverstone location is a genuine demand differentiator.

What's the process if a guest causes damage?▼

Stayful manages this entirely. Before every stay we photograph the property. After checkout we check again. If damage is found, we first attempt recovery through the Airbnb AirCover scheme (£2.5M protection). If AirCover covers it, the cost is recovered from the platform — you don't pay a penny. If the claim falls below the threshold, we use the guest security hold. Zac handles all conversations with Airbnb and the guest. You receive a report. You are never involved in difficult conversations unless you want to be.
