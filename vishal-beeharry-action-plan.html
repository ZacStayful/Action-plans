❓ Your questions — answered honestly

Are these income projections actually achievable for Warwick?▾

The figures are based on what comparable 2-bed properties within 1.5km of 39 Hill Street are actually achieving right now — not optimistic estimates. The £2,099/month average we walked through is the median for similar properties in the area. Contractors from the HS2/M4 project, hospital staff, and Warwick Castle visitors all contribute to year-round demand. That said, these are projections, not guarantees. Year 1 will be lower as reviews build — expect £1,300–£1,700/month in the first few months. Full run rate comes from month 4 onward.

What is the honest worst case if projections are not hit?▾

The honest worst case: if the property performs at 70% of projection (a significant underperformance), the STL payout would be around £1,470/month. After your £1,200 rent and ~£500 bills, you'd be roughly £230/month in the red. That's the risk of R2R — you're committed to the rent regardless of STL performance. This is why we recommend ensuring you have 3 months' rent in reserve before going live. At 85% of projection (modest underperformance), you'd be broadly break-even. At full projection you're generating £399/month profit.

What about licensing and planning permission?▾

Short-term letting a residential property to a series of guests does not constitute a material change of use and does not require planning permission in Warwick — this is not an HMO situation. As we discussed, the recommendation is to switch from council tax to business rates once you're live, which typically takes about a year to process and, for a property of this size, is likely to result in a zero rate (full small business relief). Stayful handles the day-to-day compliance for all guest-facing requirements.

Should the dining room be converted to a third bedroom?▾

As we discussed: only convert if the room is genuinely large enough to feel like a bedroom — cramped rooms hurt review scores and ADR. If it's borderline, a games room or sofa-bed living space is a better option and still allows you to accommodate more guests. Converting to a third bedroom, if viable, could increase the property's gross potential by 15–20%, but don't sacrifice comfort ratings for an extra room. One bathroom works fine for 2 bedrooms; it starts to feel tight for 3 with back-to-back guests, so a thorough review of the layout is worthwhile before committing.

How does the R2R arrangement work with the management agreement?▾

The management agreement is between Stayful and you as the operator. The fact that you're renting from the Warwick owners doesn't change how the agreement works — you sign as the responsible party. You'll need to ensure the lease from the Warwick owners explicitly permits short-term subletting (they've confirmed SA basis verbally, but you'll want this in writing before signing with us). Stayful then manages guests, pricing, cleaning, and payouts to you. Your relationship with the owners — including the £1,200/month rent — is entirely separate.

How quickly will the listing go live once we sign?▾

Typically 3–6 weeks from agreement to live, depending on how quickly the property can be furnished and photographed. The onboarding process is: agreement signed → furnishing and setup (2–4 weeks if items are ordered promptly) → thermostat and key safe installed (1 day, during setup) → professional photography (half day, plus 2–3 days editing) → listing build and review → live. Given you're expecting the property to be vacant end of June, a target go-live in mid-to-late July is realistic.
