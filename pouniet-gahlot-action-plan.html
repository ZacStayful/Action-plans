❓ Your Questions, Honestly Answered
Does a 5-bedroom house really achieve £356 per night in Reading? ▼
Yes — that's the average across comparable properties in your comp set, not a best-case figure. Properties with 5 bedrooms and 12-guest capacity command a premium because group accommodation is underserved in Reading. Your nearest comparable is achieving £45,484/year at 33% occupancy with a 4.0 star rating — with Stayful's pricing strategy and service quality, 54% occupancy at £356 ADR is a realistic projection.
What is the honest worst case if projections are not hit? ▼
At 80% of the Year 1 conservative projection, you'd net approximately £23,370. That's still 7% more than the long-let alternative. For a well-presented 5-bed in Reading with proper professional management, it's very difficult to perform below the long-let equivalent. The risk is lower than it appears because the LTL benchmark is already built into the analysis.
What happens if a guest damages something? ▼
AirCover provides up to £2.5M in host damage protection. For meaningful damage — furniture, appliances, structural — Stayful handles the entire claim. We take pre and post-stay photography at every changeover as evidence. Claims under ~£85 are typically not pursued to protect the review profile; anything significant is claimed. You will not need to deal with Airbnb directly.
How much work is required from me once we're live? ▼
Minimal. Your day-to-day involvement is essentially zero — all guest communication, check-ins, cleaning, and maintenance coordination runs through Stayful. You'll receive a monthly income statement and have read-only access to the booking calendar. The only thing we ask is that you communicate any dates you need via the platform calendar in advance.
What does the 13% vs 15% difference actually mean in money? ▼
At £70,218 gross revenue (Year 2), the 2% difference is approximately £1,404/year. Over a typical 3-year management period, that's over £4,000 saved vs the standard rate. On a 5-bed at Reading earning at this level, it's a meaningful saving.
What if I want to end the agreement — what am I committing to? ▼
Initial 6-month fixed term — early exit within this period incurs a £1,000 fee. After the initial term, it's a rolling 3-month written notice. So the maximum commitment at any point is 3 months' notice. Most owners stay because the income continues, but you're not locked in long-term.
