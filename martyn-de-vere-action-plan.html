Your questions, answered honestly
How quickly will you tell me about damage — and how is it recovered? ▼
Stayful photographs the property before and after every stay. If a guest causes damage, you'll know within 24 hours of check-out — not weeks later. We then manage the AirCover claim on your behalf. For amounts under £300, we may authorise a repair immediately to protect your next booking. For larger claims, you're notified and we coordinate. The previous experience you described — finding out too late — is exactly what pre- and post-stay documentation prevents.
How do you screen out party groups and unsuitable guests? ▼
Every booking goes through Airbnb's ID verification and booking history check. We set a £200 security deposit as standard. Properties near universities do attract younger guests on occasion, but the profile in BS3 Bedminster is predominantly short-stay professionals, medical workers, visiting families, and city-breakers. We can apply minimum-stay rules and minimum age thresholds at your request. 30% of our bookings are from repeat guests — the lowest-risk category.
Stayful has been trading since 2023 — is that enough experience? ▼
Fair question. Stayful was established for STL management in 2023 and now manages approximately 70 properties across England. The team comes from hospitality and property management backgrounds. The case studies above are real Airbnb platform data from 2025. We'd rather be a growing company where your property genuinely matters than a large agency where you're one of 3,000 clients. The Airbnb and Google reviews are the most honest reflection of how we operate day to day.
What if I decide to sell while you're managing the property? ▼
After the initial 6-month fixed term, either party can exit on 3 months' written notice. If the property sells during the initial term, the £1,000 early exit fee applies — but that's typically recovered in less than 2 months of STL income above long-let. During a sale process, actively listed STL properties often present well due to professional photography and high presentation standards. We're transparent about the practicalities if a sale becomes the most likely outcome.
What's the honest worst case if projections aren't hit? ▼
The honest worst case: a first year that underperforms the 80% conservative estimate. If occupancy tracked 10–15 points below projection — say 50–55% instead of 67% — you'd see net income around £14,000–16,000. That still matches or slightly exceeds the long-let net. The projection is based on median-aggregated data from 12 active comparables within 1.32km, so it reflects the real market, not an outlier. We don't show figures we don't believe are achievable.
What does the 6-month initial term mean in practice? ▼
The 6-month fixed term gives the listing time to build reviews, occupancy, and direct bookings — the ramp-up phase that produces the Year 2 run rate. Early exit within this term incurs a £1,000 fee. After 6 months, the agreement rolls to 3-month rolling notice — either party can exit with 3 months' written notice at any time. No lock-in beyond that. The terms are designed to be mutual, not punitive.
