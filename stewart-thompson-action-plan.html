What Drives Demand in Chester
🦁
Chester Zoo
One of the UK's most visited wildlife attractions, Chester Zoo draws over 1.5 million visitors annually. Families travelling from across the country regularly need overnight accommodation, making it a consistent year-round booking driver.
🏇
Chester Racecourse
The oldest racecourse in England, Chester hosts its flagship Race Week in May (you noted this on the call) plus multiple meetings throughout the season. Race Week alone fills the city — and you're right that it's a significant demand spike Stayful's dynamic pricing will capture.
🏛
Roman Heritage & The Rows
Chester's Roman amphitheatre, city walls, Dewa Roman Experience, and the unique medieval Rows shopping galleries are genuinely world-class heritage attractions. Cultural tourism brings visitors specifically seeking multi-night stays to explore properly.
🏥
Countess of Chester Hospital
The Countess of Chester NHS Foundation Trust is a major regional employer and generates visiting families, medical professionals, and contract workers seeking short-stay accommodation year-round — exactly the high-value, mid-length guest profile that protects occupancy in shoulder months.
🎓
University of Chester
Chester's university attracts visiting academics, prospective families, and visiting lecturers throughout the academic year. Combined with the university's multiple campuses and research partnerships, this adds consistent demand outside tourist peak months.
🚂
Excellent Connectivity
Chester has direct rail services to London Euston (via Crewe), Manchester, Liverpool, and North Wales. This positions the city as an attractive base for business visitors and those wanting a quiet city stay with easy connections — boosting direct and corporate bookings.

Your Questions — Answered Honestly
How confident should I be that the cleaning standard will be maintained consistently?
This was your biggest stated concern on the call, and it's a fair one. Stayful uses a roster of vetted local cleaning contractors in Chester who are centrally coordinated and quality-checked. Clean scores directly affect your Airbnb listing performance and Stayful's revenue — the incentive alignment is strong. For a 5-bed, turnovers will be more intensive than a 1-2 bed flat, and Stayful prices cleaning to reflect this. If a clean falls below standard, it gets flagged and remediated before the next guest — you'll see this in your monthly statement and Slack channel.
You're comparing three management companies — what should I actually be looking at?
The right comparison points are: occupancy and ADR data for actual properties in Chester (not just projections), whether they have a no-stag/hen policy, how they handle damage claims, what their direct booking capability is (this reduces platform dependency), and how the owner communication works day-to-day. The competitor screen Zac is sending covers the key differences. Fee rate alone is a misleading comparison — a company charging 13% but generating lower occupancy will net you less than 15% with higher performance.
What's the honest worst case if the income projections aren't hit in Year 1?
The worst realistic case for a well-presented 5-bed in Chester CH1 is approximately 70–75% of projections in Year 1 — so roughly £27,500–£29,500 net. That's still broadly comparable to or above current long-let income (you're currently earning ~£30,000/year from students). In a genuine downside scenario — property doesn't photograph well, reviews start slowly — you're likely looking at parity, not a loss. You can exit after 6 months if the numbers aren't working. We don't know any of this in advance, but the analysis is based on real comparable properties in Chester and we'll give you an honest assessment after 3 months.
I have two students who want to stay on — how do I handle that alongside this decision?
You have a 2-week window to decide (as you said on the call), which aligns with needing to sort student contracts. If you're proceeding with STL: give students notice now, allow the natural tenancy end, then onboarding takes 2–3 weeks from agreement signing. If you want to honour one more student year, Stayful can plan the transition for when they leave — just let Zac know the timeline. The contract start date is flexible.
What happens with Chester Race Week — is it really worth more than the June–August data suggests?
You made a good point on the call. Race Week (typically mid-May) is one of the highest-demand periods in Chester's hospitality calendar and it does create a spike. The Stayful analysis uses seasonal average data for May, which somewhat smooths the Race Week premium. In practice, Stayful's dynamic pricing will push rates significantly higher during that specific week, capturing above-average nightly rates. So the May projection is, if anything, a slight understatement of peak Race Week potential.
What are the contract terms I should understand before signing?
6-month initial fixed term — exiting before that costs £1,000 (a genuine pre-estimate of onboarding costs). After 6 months: 3-month rolling notice from either party. During the term: Stayful has sole discretion on pricing, platforms, and listing content — you can't adjust it unilaterally (this protects the listing performance). Owner access to the property requires 72 hours notice and prior written consent via Slack. Full details in the agreement linked above.
