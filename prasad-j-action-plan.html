❓ Your Questions — Answered Directly
Q
"What happens in the worst months — can I cover the mortgage in January and March?"
January and February gross ~£1,577 each in Year 1. Net ~£825 doesn't fully cover your £1,250 monthly costs — you'd be roughly £425 short in those months. Annually you're cash-flow positive (£17,280 net vs £15,000 costs). Keep a small cash buffer for Q1 — the summer months more than compensate. This is the honest reality of STL in the slower months.
Q
"Will occupancy actually hit 60%?"
60% is the Year 2 target at full run-rate. Year 1 at 80% reflects the ramp. At 50% occupancy, gross would be ~£32,850 — still above long-let. Your garden, 2-car parking, and workspace give you advantages most city-centre flats don't have. Competitors averaged 40 reviews and 4.4 rating — below what Stayful targets.
Q
"How do I use the property for my daughter when she's at Manchester University?"
You can block any dates via the owner portal or through Slack — confirmed guest bookings always take priority. The recommendation: block low-season months (Jan–Mar) for personal use, keep July and August available. A blocked peak-season week costs £700–£900 in foregone gross. Give Stayful as much advance notice as possible.
Q
"What's the honest worst case if projections aren't hit?"
At 40% occupancy, annual gross ~£26,280 and net ~£13,760 — below your running costs. That's the real downside. The mitigation: 803 Eastbank Tower in Manchester (a 1-bed flat) grossed £56,921 in 2025. Your 3-bed detached has a wider audience. Stayful wouldn't take this on if we weren't confident — but the risk is real and worth acknowledging.
Q
"How does switching from long-let help with the new tenant rights legislation?"
Short-let guests operate under a licence, not a tenancy. The Renters' Rights Act 2025 — which strengthens tenant possession rights and effectively ends Section 21 — does not apply to short-let arrangements. This is a genuine structural advantage for landlords moving away from long-let.
Q
"What about business rates vs council tax?"
Properties let as furnished holiday accommodation for 140+ nights per year can qualify for small business rates relief — in many cases meaning zero rates payable. Stayful guides you through this as part of onboarding. It's worth doing early and correctly.
