What drives demand in SE25 — South Norwood
⚽
Crystal Palace FC — Selhurst Park
Selhurst Park is literally in SE25. Home fixtures bring thousands of visiting fans who need accommodation within walking distance. Premier League & Championship games drive strong weekend demand throughout the football season (August–May).
🏥
NHS & Healthcare Workers
Croydon University Hospital and several South London NHS trusts are nearby. Travelling healthcare professionals and contractors are a significant weekday booking source — exactly the guest type that fills your quieter midweek slots.
🏢
Croydon Business Hub
Croydon is one of the UK's largest commercial districts outside Central London. Corporate contractors, relocators and business travellers use SE25 as a cost-effective base — providing consistent weekday demand.
🚂
Central London Access
Norwood Junction station gives guests London Bridge in ~25 minutes and Blackfriars in ~30. Guests get Central London access at South London prices — a compelling value proposition for both leisure and business travellers.
🌿
Crystal Palace Park & Area Appeal
One of South London's largest Victorian parks is a 10-minute walk. Events, the National Sports Centre, and the park's year-round appeal attract weekend leisure visitors looking for a greener base than central London.
📉
Low Competition Density
SE25 has relatively few well-managed short-let properties compared to nearby areas — meaning less competition for bookings. Combined with high direct booking potential for this area, a well-presented property stands out strongly.

📋 Property Readiness Checklist — Essential vs Non-Essential
Exactly what you asked for on the call — what you need vs what's nice to have.
✅ ESSENTIAL — Required before listing
ESSENTIAL
Smoke detectors — Legally required. Minimum one per floor. If you don't already have working ones, fit them before the property goes live. ~£15–£25 each.
ESSENTIAL
Carbon monoxide alarm — Legally required if any gas appliances are present (boiler, hob, oven). ~£20–£30.
ESSENTIAL
Fire blanket — Recommended in kitchen. Low cost (~£10–£15), expected by guests, and good practice for all short-let properties.
ESSENTIAL
Smart thermostat — Essential for remote management. Stayful can set it between stays and guests can control their comfort. Hive or Nest. ~£150–£200 one-time. We discussed this on the call.
ESSENTIAL
Key safe — For self-check-in. A coded key safe means Stayful can manage key handover remotely. ~£30–£60. We can advise on placement.
❌ NON-ESSENTIAL — Not required, not recommended
NOT NEEDED
Security cameras inside the property — Airbnb prohibits interior cameras. Even exterior cameras are optional and most Stayful hosts don't use them. We discussed this — don't worry about it.
NOT NEEDED
PIR / motion sensors — Not required for short-let. Some hosts fit them in car parks or communal areas but they're not part of a standard setup and aren't expected by guests or platforms.
💡 Bottom line: Smoke detectors, CO alarm, fire blanket, smart thermostat, key safe. That's it. Total cost roughly £260–£320 if you need everything. You'll likely already have most of these.

Frequently Asked Questions
Do I need to stop selling while I'm with Stayful?+
Absolutely not. The agreement runs on a 3-month rolling notice period after the initial 6-month term. If you accept an offer, give 3 months' notice and that's it. Stayful will work around any viewings you need to arrange — just coordinate through the Slack channel with at least 72 hours' notice.
Will having short-let guests affect my ability to get viewings?+
It actually works in your favour. The property is always clean, freshly staged, and professionally presented — far better than a long-let tenant situation. Viewings can be arranged through Stayful with 72 hours' notice. Buyers typically respond well to a furnished, well-kept property.
What if guests damage the property?+
Airbnb's AirCover provides up to £2.5M in host damage protection — Stayful manages all claims on your behalf. We photograph the property before and after every stay, and pursue any damage through Airbnb's resolution centre. The vast majority of stays have zero issues. For anything under £300, we can authorise repairs without your input to keep things moving.
What if I don't get the income projected — what's the honest worst case?+
Year 1 will be below Year 2 projections — that's expected and built into the £10,480 conservative figure. If STL performance were to track significantly below projection in Year 2+, your exit is straightforward: 3-month notice and you switch to long-let (£12,848/year) or sell. You're not locked in. The risk is low because your downside is essentially the long-let rate.
I'm currently getting weekends but not weekdays — will that improve?+
Yes — and this is exactly where proper management makes the difference. Booking.com drives significant weekday corporate and healthcare bookings. We also use dynamic weekday pricing to compete for NHS workers, business contractors and relocators. Most properties see weekday occupancy materially improve within the first 2–3 months once we've listed on all platforms and tuned pricing.
What are my legal responsibilities as a short-let host?+
You need valid gas and electrical safety certificates, working smoke and CO alarms, and buildings/contents insurance that covers short-let use. Stayful helps guide you through these during onboarding. The checklist in this action plan covers what's needed for the property itself. Council tax and utilities remain in your name throughout.
