What drives demand in Oakwood, Derby

🏭
Rolls-Royce Aerospace (Derby)

Rolls-Royce's global aerospace HQ is in Derby, employing 10,000+ people. The site constantly brings in contractors, visiting engineers, supply chain staff and international specialists — many needing accommodation for days or weeks at a time.

🚗
Toyota Manufacturing UK (Burnaston)

Toyota's UK assembly plant at Burnaston is just 10km from Oakwood. It regularly brings Japanese engineers, technical supervisors and supply chain partners for stints of one to several weeks — a reliable source of longer, higher-value bookings.

🏥
Royal Derby Hospital

One of the East Midlands' major NHS hospitals, within commuting distance of Oakwood. Visiting medical professionals, patient families travelling from out of town, and locum specialists all generate consistent demand for short stays.

🎓
University of Derby

A growing university with campuses across the city. Visiting academics, prospective students and their parents, and short-course attendees create reliable off-season demand that balances the property's seasonal curve.

🚆
Transport Links & Connectivity

Derby station sits on the Midland Main Line with direct connections to London St Pancras, Sheffield and Nottingham. Easy rail access makes the property attractive to corporate travellers who want to avoid hotel pricing.

📅
Events & Local Calendar

Pride Park (Derby County FC), Derby Racecourse, and the city's events calendar bring leisure guests during peak months. Combined with the corporate base, this creates a mixed-occupancy profile that drives year-round bookings.

You asked about Rolls-Royce and Toyota corporate bookings. These companies' staff initially book through Airbnb and Booking.com — but once Stayful has established a relationship (typically within 4–6 months), many convert to direct bookings. That's cheaper for the guest and more profitable for you. Oakwood's proximity to both sites makes this a realistic outcome here.

Your questions — answered honestly

The box room is only 2.1 × 2.1m — does that affect the income figures?▼
No — and we addressed this on the call. By adding a sofa bed in the living room, you reach 6-guest capacity, which keeps your figures comparable to the projections. The box room works as a single, and the zip-and-link in the second bedroom gives you flexibility between double and twin. Most bookings for 3–4 guests won't even use the box room — it's a bonus bed, not a limiting factor.
Is £173 per night realistic? I was thinking more like £120.▼
£173 is the property rate — not per person. A group of 4 staying at £173/night is £43 each, which is excellent value compared to a hotel. This rate is derived from existing comparable properties within 3.5km of Oakwood, so it reflects what the local market actually supports today — not a projection. The low local competition (average 15 reviews, 4.5 rating) means you have genuine room to meet or exceed this.
What does the ramp-up period look like? It won't go straight to full figures.▼
Correct — and we showed this honestly in the Year 1 projections above. Until you reach 10 reviews, expect 5–10% below the forecast figure. Between 10 and 15 reviews, you hit stability and the full projected rates. Above 15 reviews with a strong rating, you have pricing power — you can often exceed the forecast by 5–10% because you're now the best-ranked property in the area. First bookings typically come within 24–72 hours of going live. Ten reviews usually follow within 2–3 months.
What's the honest worst case if projections are not hit?▼
Your mortgage is £400/month. Even in February — the lowest month — the conservative Year 1 estimate is around £790 net. That's still nearly double your mortgage. The long-let alternative is £1,100/month. So the true worst case for a month like February is roughly equivalent to long-let income. The difference is that in peak months (July: est. £1,822 Year 1, £2,277 Year 2), you're significantly ahead. You're not taking on risk of a loss — you're trading some monthly predictability for significantly higher annual return.
Do we need different insurance?▼
No. Your existing building/landlord insurance continues. Because Stayful manages all guest-related activity, your insurer treats this as standard managed letting. If anything changes in your policy on renewal, it's something to flag then — but in our experience running Derby properties since early 2024, it hasn't been an issue. AirCover (£2.5M damage protection) sits on top of your existing cover regardless.
Can the property stay on the market while we short-let?▼
Yes — and this is one of the most practical advantages of the Stayful model. You control the booking calendar. Whenever you have viewings scheduled, you block those dates via the app. No bookings are taken during that window. Guests never know the property is for sale, and you continue earning income between viewings. If the property sells, you give notice under the standard contract terms. There's no conflict between the two.
