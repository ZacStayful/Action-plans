Your Questions Answered
Can I really restrict guests to weekday-only lets?+
Yes — Stayful can work with a weekday-only model. The honest trade-off is that weekend bookings (especially Friday and Saturday nights) represent a meaningful share of monthly gross. Zac's recommendation was to start with a Minut occupancy and noise sensor rather than a blanket weekday restriction: it gives you real-time visibility of guest behaviour, flags any noise breaches, and lets you refine the approach based on actual data rather than a blanket rule that limits income from the start.
What happens with Bristol's proposed 90-day short-let cap?+
As of today, Bristol's proposed 90-day restriction is still in the planning/consultation stage and is not in force. The only enforceable limit in England is Greater London's 90-day rule. If Bristol does introduce a cap, Stayful will manage compliance — and historically, councils move from proposal to licensing (rather than outright bans), so properties already operating are typically grandfathered in.
My management company and neighbours may object — is that a deal-breaker?+
It's a real consideration, not a deal-breaker for many clients. The approach that works best: explore whether your lease permits short-term use (many residential leases are silent on this). Setting up a limited company with a tenancy agreement between you personally and your trading entity is a common and effective structure. Zac uses the same approach himself. Worth taking legal advice on your specific lease before committing.
What's the honestly worst case if projections aren't hit?+
Year 1 is always the most variable. The figures shown use a conservative 80% multiplier, but a genuinely poor first year — slow review build, a few cancellations, a quiet month — could mean net income 50-60% of the run-rate projection. For a property like yours with low setup cost and no mortgage reliance, the downside is measured in months of below-expectation income rather than a loss. The key lever is speed: getting great photography, a well-written listing, and the first 10 reviews quickly sets the trajectory.
How does the Ltd company structure work for the lease?+
You own the property personally. You set up a trading limited company (or use an existing one). The limited company signs a tenancy agreement with you — you're effectively the landlord to your own company. The company then operates the short-let service. This keeps the freehold in your personal name, allows the company to apply for business rates via the VOA (removing the double council tax risk), and is a recognised and widely-used structure in the STL industry.
What is the contract commitment and exit terms?+
Six-month initial fixed term. Early exit during this term incurs a £1,000 fee (or the value of confirmed bookings remaining, whichever is higher). After the initial term, either party can give three months' notice in writing. The three-month notice period is mainly to service guests who have already booked — Stayful won't cancel confirmed reservations. In practice, most owners who want to exit can do so smoothly within a quarter.
